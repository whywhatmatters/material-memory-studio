## Brand Core

- **What Material Memory Studio is:** A private research studio and salon in Seoul at the intersection of art, coaching, and material research — not within any single field. By appointment; gradual, intentional opening.
- **Who it is for:** Artists, designers, founders, practitioners, researchers, and professionals drawn to careful thinking and conversation that resists easy resolution. People in transition, in-between roles, identities, disciplines, or life chapters.
- **What makes it distinct:** Four pillars (Artist Studio, Salon, Coaching, Material Research); Border Crossing Sessions as a distinct coaching format; material-led inquiry; held, unhurried dialogue rather than consultation or performance.
- **How art / coaching / material research / technology relate:** Art and prints from studio practice and nomadic archive; coaching (including BCS) is reflective and human-led; material research is a core axis (archive, biomaterial, regional materials); technology/AI is a supporting tool (e.g. reflection print), not the center of the experience.

## Border Crossing Sessions Canonical Copy

- **Page purpose:** Detail page for Border Crossing Sessions; inform and convert to booking; premium, reflective, non-corporate tone.
- **Who it is for:** People in-between (roles, identities, countries, disciplines, life chapters); artists, designers, researchers, founders, professionals moving across disciplines/geographies/life chapters; those who resist easy categorisation; Seoul-based or able to come in person.
- **Session premise:** Distinct coaching format; grounded in BORDER Framework™; held, unhurried conversation at the pace of genuine inquiry; not a consultation, not a performance. Helps identify the border you are standing in (psychological, professional, relational, geographic, economic) rather than rushing toward clarity.
- **Session options / pricing:** 60 min USD 180; 90 min USD 260. Introductory pilot pricing. Format = 60- or 90-minute one-on-one; Saturdays, by appointment; in-person at Material Memory Studio in Seoul; English or Korean.
- **Key difference 60 vs 90 min:** 60 min = shorter; mini coaching; reflection print in concise form at close. 90 min = more symbolic exploration and collaborative refinement of reflection language; time to refine statement together before printing; distinct step for AI-assisted reflection statement creation.
- **Outputs / takeaways:** Two outputs. (1) Border Crossing Portrait — symbolic portrait, body part or personal object, threshold; photographed in studio, printed on site. (2) Border Crossing Reflection Print — personalized from session conversation, refined in-studio with AI as supporting tool; may include one-line threshold statement, brief reflection sheet, or conceptual print (image + language).
- **Role of AI:** At close, words distilled in-studio with AI as supporting tool — not as the center of the experience, but to help you leave with a more tangible reflection of your crossing.
- **Booking tone:** First sessions are available now; select a Saturday on the booking page; both session lengths offered. Small, intentional opening; not a large-scale launch.

## Tone Guardrails

- **Do:** Elegant, restrained, reflective, intelligent, calm. Precise (by appointment, Saturdays, in-person Seoul, limited pilot). Warm but not casual.
- **Do not:** Overly commercial (sign up now, don’t miss out, countdowns). Startup-salesy (disrupt, unlock, join thousands). "AI product" language (AI-powered, AI-driven, AI experience — use "AI-assisted" / "supporting tool" only, and subordinate). Therapeutic overpromising. Vague luxury without substance (generic "curated", "premium" without meaning).

## Bilingual Guidance

- **English:** Editorial, calm, refined. Sentences can be longer where they serve clarity; rhythm and italics matter. U.S. English.
- **Korean:** Natural, not overly literal or awkwardly translated. Avoid stiff or corporate Korean. Preserve conceptual clarity in both languages.
- **Brand terms:** Keep in English where branded: "Border Crossing Sessions", "BORDER Framework™", "Material Memory Studio". Do not translate these into Korean unless there is an established Korean equivalent already in use on the site.
