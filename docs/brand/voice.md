# Material Memory Studio — Brand Voice

## Brand positioning

Material Memory Studio is a **private research studio and salon** in Seoul. It sits at the intersection of art, coaching, and material research — not within any single field. It is intentional, gradual, and invitation-based (by appointment). The brand should feel like a held space for serious inquiry, not a product or a mass offering.

## Core themes

- **Materials, memory, practice** — Where physical/material culture meets reflection and making.
- **Border-crossing** — Thresholds between disciplines, places, selves, or seasons of life; complexity held without premature resolution.
- **Careful thinking** — Unhurried conversation, resistance to easy categorisation, clarity that emerges from inquiry rather than from pressure.
- **Making and dialogue** — Art, prints, coaching, and research as intertwined; the studio is both a place of making and a place of conversation.

## Tone of voice

- **Elegant, restrained, reflective** — No hype, no hard sell.
- **Precise** — Use specific language (e.g. "by appointment", "limited pilot", "in-person in Seoul") rather than vague promises.
- **Warm but not casual** — Inviting to those who are already drawn to depth; not trying to appeal to everyone.
- **Confident in incompleteness** — Okay naming what is "in development", "upcoming", or "gradually opening"; no need to sound fully finished.

## What the writing should feel like

- Reading the copy should feel like being invited into a considered space.
- Sentences can be longer and more layered where they serve clarity; rhythm matters.
- Italics are used for emphasis and for a more reflective tone (e.g. *practice meet*, *as it unfolds*).
- The studio speaks in first person only where it makes sense (e.g. "we welcome"); often the third person or passive is used for description ("hosted by Jay Lee", "shaped by four pillars").

## What to avoid in copy

- **Overly commercial language** — No "sign up now", "don't miss out", countdowns, or pressure.
- **Corporate or generic** — No "solutions", "leverage", "synergy", or buzzwords.
- **Making AI the center** — The experience is human-led; AI is a supporting tool. Avoid framing the studio or Border Crossing Sessions as an "AI product" or "AI experience."
- **Over-translation in Korean** — Korean should feel natural and readable, not stiff or obviously translated from English. Avoid corporate Korean tone.
- **Vague openness** — Prefer "by appointment", "Saturdays", "limited pilot" over "opening soon" without specifics.

## Preferred vocabulary

| Use | Avoid |
|-----|--------|
| Border-crossing, threshold | Pivot, transition (unless in a specific coaching context) |
| Session, format, offering | Product, package |
| By appointment, appointment-based | Booking (ok in UI), sign-up |
| Reflection, reflective | Self-help, coaching (as a buzzword) |
| Material research, material-led | Materials (as a generic topic) |
| Studio, salon | Space (overused), hub |
| Hosted by Jay Lee | Run by, owned by |
| In-person (Seoul) | On-site, offline (unless contrasting with remote) |

## How to talk about…

### Coaching

- Frame as **reflective**, **held**, **unhurried** — "a space to think slowly and speak without forced resolution."
- Border Crossing Sessions are **distinct** within the studio: "a distinct coaching format", "reflective sessions for those navigating thresholds."
- Not a consultation; not a performance. Coaching is part of the studio's pillars alongside Artist Studio, Salon, and Material Research.
- Link to **whywhatmatters.com** for coaching essays, reflections, and newsletter — keep that as the written/ongoing layer; the studio is the in-person, session-based layer.

### Art / works / prints

- **Original artworks and photographic prints** from "ongoing studio practice and nomadic archive."
- Materials and media are named specifically: painting, sculpture, glass, ceramics, bioplastics, beeswax, alternative photography, print-based works.
- Theme: "how materials hold memory across time, place, and transformation."
- **jayartmaking.com/works** is for selected works and extended archive — separate from the studio site but clearly linked.

### Material research

- "Material archive, biomaterial exploration, regional materials."
- "Evolving archive" — part studio library, part working index.
- Shaped by "memory, time, transformation, and place."
- Can be "in development"; no need to oversell.

### AI as a supporting tool

- Use **"AI-assisted"** or **"AI as a supporting tool"** sparingly and only where relevant (e.g. reflection print, reflection statement).
- Always subordinate to the human experience: "not as the center of the experience, but as a way to help you leave with a more tangible reflection."
- Do **not** lead with AI in positioning or headlines. The studio is not an "AI-assisted" or "AI-powered" brand.

## Bilingual guidance

### English

- Should feel **elegant, restrained, reflective**.
- Serif for key phrases (in design); sentence structure can be slightly literary.
- U.S. English spelling and conventions (e.g. "practice" for noun).

### Korean

- Should **not** sound over-translated or corporate.
- Natural, readable Korean; avoid stiff honorifics or formal corporate tone unless context demands it.
- Keep proper nouns in English where they are branded: "Border Crossing Sessions", "BORDER Framework™", "Material Memory Studio."
- "경계 넘기" is used for border-crossing where it fits; "자세히 보기" for "Explore / see more" type CTAs.

## Summary

- **Position:** Private studio and salon; art, coaching, material research; by appointment; Seoul.
- **Tone:** Elegant, precise, warm, confident in incompleteness.
- **Avoid:** Commercial pressure, corporate buzzwords, making AI the hero, vague or over-translated Korean.
- **AI:** Mention only as supporting tool; never center the brand or the experience on AI.
