# Content rules — Material Memory Studio

Brand and copy guidance for all text on the site. Align with `docs/brand/voice.md`; these rules are the short reference for edits.

---

## Tone

- **Elegant, restrained, reflective, intelligent, calm.** The site should feel like an invitation into a considered space, not a sales page.
- **Precise** — Use specific language ("by appointment", "Saturdays", "limited pilot", "in-person in Seoul") instead of vague promises.
- **Warm but not casual** — For people drawn to depth and careful thinking.

---

## What to avoid

- **Startup/landing clichés** — No "disrupt", "reimagine", "unlock", "join thousands", hero stats, or generic value props.
- **Aggressive sales language** — No "Sign up now", "Don't miss out", countdowns, scarcity tricks, or pressure.
- **AI as the product** — The site and Border Crossing Sessions are **human-led**. AI is a **supporting tool** (e.g. for reflection print). Do not describe the studio or offerings as "AI-powered", "AI-driven", or an "AI experience". Use "AI-assisted" or "AI as a supporting tool" only where relevant and always subordinate to the human experience.
- **Corporate or generic** — No "solutions", "leverage", "synergy", "ecosystem", or buzzwords.
- **Over-explained art** — Art and prints are described in a materially grounded way; avoid over-justifying or sounding like a gallery blurb.

---

## Coaching language

- **Held, thoughtful, non-corporate.** "A space to think slowly and speak without forced resolution."
- Border Crossing Sessions: "reflective sessions", "distinct coaching format", "BORDER Framework™". Not a consultation; not a performance.
- **whywhatmatters.com** = coaching essays, reflections, newsletter — use that wording when linking.

---

## Art / works / prints language

- **Materially grounded.** "Original artworks and photographic prints"; "ongoing studio practice and nomadic archive"; "how materials hold memory across time, place, and transformation."
- Name media when relevant: painting, sculpture, glass, ceramics, bioplastics, beeswax, alternative photography, print-based works.
- **jayartmaking.com/works** = selected works and extended archive — use that wording when linking.

---

## Preferred wording (use consistently)

| Concept | Prefer | Avoid |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Studio identity | Material Memory Studio; private studio and salon | space, hub |
| Offerings | session, format, offering; by appointment | product, package, sign-up |
| Coaching | Border Crossing Sessions; BORDER Framework™; reflective, held | pivot, transition (as default), self-help |
| Location | in-person in Seoul; Material Memory Studio in Seoul | on-site, offline |
| Access | by appointment; appointment-based | booking (except in UI like "Book…") |
| Host | Hosted by Jay Lee | Run by, owned by |
| AI | AI-assisted; AI as a supporting tool; not the center of the experience | AI-powered, AI-driven, AI experience |

---

## English

- **Editorial and refined.** Sentences can be longer where they serve clarity; rhythm and italics for emphasis matter.
- U.S. English. Serif (Cormorant Garamond) is used for key phrases in the design; copy should match that tone.

---

## Korean

- **Natural, not literal or awkwardly translated.** Avoid stiff or corporate Korean; avoid obvious calques from English.
- Keep **proper nouns** in English where they are branded: "Border Crossing Sessions", "BORDER Framework™", "Material Memory Studio."
- When in doubt, prefer readability and natural phrasing over word-for-word translation.
